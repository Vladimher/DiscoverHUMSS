Your Course Option

The Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) strand serves as a gateway to a wide array of course options in college, each designed to prepare students for fulfilling careers in fields that prioritize critical thinking, communication, and the study of human society and culture. Here are some of the many courses HUMSS graduates can pursue, along with their respective descriptions:

Bachelor of Arts in Communication

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ This course focuses on developing effective communication skills in various media platforms, including journalism, broadcasting, and public relations. Students learn to craft compelling messages and manage information dissemination in diverse industries.

Bachelor of Science in Psychology

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ A course that delves into human behavior and mental processes, equipping students with the skills to analyze, assess, and assist individuals in improving their mental well-being. It is also a strong foundation for pursuing a career in clinical psychology, counseling, or human resources.

Bachelor of Science in Social Work

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ This program trains students to address social issues and improve the quality of life of individuals, families, and communities. It combines theoretical knowledge and practical fieldwork to build compassionate and competent social workers.

Bachelor of Arts in Political Science

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ Students explore government systems, political theories, and public policies. This course prepares them for careers in governance, law, or international relations by developing their analytical and decision-making skills.

Bachelor of Science in Criminology

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ A course that prepares students to understand criminal behavior, law enforcement procedures, and the justice system. Graduates often pursue careers as police officers, forensic analysts, or crime investigators.

Bachelor of Arts in Literature

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ This course immerses students in the study of literary works across cultures and eras. It develops skills in critical analysis, creative writing, and cultural understanding, making graduates suitable for careers in writing, editing, or academia.

Bachelor of Elementary Education

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ Ideal for aspiring educators, this course equips students with teaching strategies and methodologies to become effective elementary school teachers. It includes hands-on training and exposure to classroom environments.

Bachelor of Secondary Education (Major in English, Social Studies, or Filipino)

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ This program trains future high school teachers specializing in their chosen fields. It emphasizes lesson planning, classroom management, and effective teaching techniques.

Bachelor of Science in Tourism Management

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ A course designed for those interested in the tourism and hospitality industry. Students learn about travel operations, destination marketing, and event management, preparing them for roles as tour guides, travel agents, or tourism officers.

Bachelor of Arts in Philosophy

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ This program focuses on exploring philosophical concepts, logic, and ethics. It sharpens critical thinking and reasoning skills, often leading to careers in academia, writing, or law.

Bachelor of Arts in Sociology

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ A study of society, social interactions, and cultural phenomena. It prepares students for careers in social research, community development, or policy analysis.

Bachelor of Science in International Studies

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ This course examines global issues, foreign relations, and international policies, ideal for students aiming for careers in diplomacy, foreign service, or global business.

Bachelor of Arts in Journalism

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ Students are trained to research, write, and report news and stories across various media platforms. It fosters integrity and professionalism for a career in journalism or media.

Bachelor of Science in Development Communication

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ A multidisciplinary program that combines communication skills with social development, empowering students to create effective campaigns for societal change.

Bachelor of Science in Public Administration

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ This course prepares students for managing public resources, implementing policies, and leading government or non-profit organizations.

Bachelor of Arts in History

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ A course for those passionate about exploring past events and their relevance to contemporary society. It develops research and analytical skills useful in teaching, archiving, or heritage management.

Bachelor of Fine Arts (Major in Creative Writing or Visual Arts)

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ A program for students inclined toward the arts, honing their creative talents in writing or visual expression for careers in literature, design, or advertising.

Bachelor of Science in Economics

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ This course covers economic theories, financial systems, and policy analysis, preparing students for careers in banking, market research, or consultancy.

Bachelor of Science in Guidance and Counseling

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ Students are trained to provide guidance and support to individuals facing academic, career, or personal challenges. It includes psychological assessments and intervention planning.

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ These courses highlight the versatility of the HUMSS strand, opening opportunities in education, media, public service, arts, and many other sectors. Each program fosters skills and knowledge tailored to various interests and career goals.
